Mental Health Issues

Underage users are more vulnerable to the pressures of social media, including cyberbullying, unrealistic beauty standards, and constant comparison.

This can lead to anxiety, depression, and low self-esteem, especially during key stages of development.

They may begin to link their self-worth to likes, comments, and followers, which creates a cycle of validation-seeking behaviour.

Over time, this can make them emotionally dependent on social media for confidence.

Exposure to Inappropriate Content

Social media platforms are not always able to filter harmful or mature content.

Young users might come across violent, sexual, or misleading information, which can distort their understanding of the world or influence risky behaviour.

Without knowing how to critically evaluate what they see, they can absorb harmful ideas or even imitate dangerous trends.

This exposure can shape their beliefs, attitudes, and behaviour in unsafe ways.
