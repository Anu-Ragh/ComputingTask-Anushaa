Preventing underage people from misusing social media starts with awareness and smart boundaries. First, parents and guardians should be actively involved — this means setting age-appropriate restrictions, using parental control tools, and having open conversations about online safety.

Social media apps should also enforce age limits properly by improving their age verification processes. Schools can help by teaching digital literacy — kids need to know how to recognise scams, protect their privacy, and understand that not everything online is real.

Finally, it’s important to encourage real-life activities and balance. Too much time online can affect mental health, sleep, and relationships. By promoting offline hobbies and setting screen time rules, we create a safer, healthier environment for young people to grow up in — both online and offline.
